GMC SUPPORTS OPERATION TULI: A SUCCESSFUL PARTNERSHIP FOR YOUTH HEALTH

May 20, 2025 | Lapu-Lapu City

Share
Share
Post

In support of the LGU initiative, General Milling Corporation (GMC), in collaboration with the
Lapu-Lapu City Health Office headed by Dr. Agnes C. Realiza successfully held an Operation Tuli last
May 20, 2025, at Lapu-Lapu City, providing free and safe circumcision services to young members of
the community.

This collaborative effort underscores GMC’s commitment to Corporate Social Responsibility and
community health initiatives. By providing financial support, GMC helped ensure the smooth operation
of the event, contributing to the well-being and hygiene of the youth in Lapu-Lapu City.

Mr. Edwin Y. Kho, GMC Finance Director, was visibly present during the event, demonstrating the
company’s leadership commitment. He was accompanied by EHS personnel Mrs. Sarah Jane Sepe, GMC Head
Nurse, and Mr. Francis Dave Doblas, PCO/Safety Officer, who actively supported the health team on
site. Nurse IV, Mrs. Grace Mary Chan Carungay, Mr. Ferdinand Tepait, CHO’s division chief for
environmental and sanitation and their dedicated team also played a crucial role in coordinating and
facilitating the event, ensuring everything ran efficiently and safely.

OPERATION TULI, supported by GMC and the local health office, highlights how public-private
cooperation can make meaningful impacts on public health and youth development.
