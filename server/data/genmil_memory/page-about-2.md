ABOUT GENERAL MILLING CORPORATION

THROUGH INNOVATION AND QUALITY, GMC PROVIDES SUPERIOR PRODUCTS THAT ENHANCE YOUR FOOD PRODUCTION AND
ENSURE EXCEPTIONAL TASTE.

General Milling Corporation is one of the largest integrated food companies in the Philippines,
built on our core values of quality, innovation, teamwork, and the vision of becoming a leading food
company. Over six decades, our ability to adapt to your changing needs has driven our stability and
growth. We invest in modern R&D facilities and application laboratories, where our trained
specialists foster creativity and innovation. We are dedicated to building sustainable partnerships
with our suppliers, creating mutually beneficial relationships with our customers, and maintaining a
healthy work environment for our employees.

CERTIFIED MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS

Upholding the highest global standards, GMC is committed to strengthening consumer confidence,
ensuring full regulatory compliance, and continuously enhancing food safety and quality processes
across the organization. Guided by excellence and integrity, we deliver products you can trust — in
every meal, every moment, every milestone.

OUR FEEDS SAFETY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM IS CERTIFIED TO ISO 22000:2018, REFLECTING OUR DEDICATION TO
PROTECTING ANIMAL HEALTH AND ENSURING SAFE FOOD FROM FARM TO TABLE.

CERTIFIED UNDER THE FSSC 22000, WE ARE DEDICATED TO CONSISTENTLY PRODUCE SAFE QUALITY WHEAT FLOUR
AND BAKERY INGREDIENTS THAT MEET INTERNATIONAL STANDARDS AND REFLECT GLOBAL BEST PRACTICES IN FOOD

OUR FLOUR AND BAKERY PRODUCTS ARE PROUDLY HALAL CERTIFIED, GIVING YOU THE CONFIDENCE THAT EVERY BAG
IS PRODUCED IN FULL COMPLIANCE WITH ISLAMIC LAWS, INTERNATIONAL HALAL STANDARDS, AND STRICT FOOD
SAFETY PRACTICES.

OUR

SOCIAL RESPONSIBILITIES

GMC is committed to making positive and sustainable impacts in the communities we serve. Our
corporate social responsibility (CSR) efforts focus on environmental protection and enhancing
education.
Our team actively participates in initiatives such as mangrove tree planting and coastal clean-ups
in Mactan and surrounding islands. These efforts help protect shorelines, preserve water quality,
save marine life, and improve community well-being.
In education, GMC partners with Silliman University’s College of Agriculture to advance Animal
Science. We have established the SU – G.K. Young Feed Laboratory with essential tools for feed
testing and provide ongoing training and practical feed trial experiences to support Animal Science
students at Silliman University.

DISCOVER

OUR HISTORY

1985
June 236th, 1958

FOUNDER

Registered with the Securities and Exchange Commission as a Corporation primarily to engage in flour
milling.

1961
Flour mill plant established

FLOUR MILL PLANT, LAPU-LAPU

Completion of first flour mill in Lapu-lapu City, Cebu, and start of operations.

1962

EXPANDED TO OTHER INTEGRATED OPERATIONS

GMC expanded to other integrated operations such as feed milling, maize milling, soybean milling,
yeast production, livestock raising and processing, and manufacture of various complementary
consumer products such as pasta, instant noodles, snack foods, coffee and cooking oils.

1965

CORN MILL PLANT ESTABLISHED

GMC established a corn mill in Lapu-Lapu Cebu.

1966

LUZON EXPANSION

GMC began its operation in Luzon with the establishment of Agri-business, focusing on piggery &
poultry. This was also the year when it started the manufacture of fermented products, with the
incorporation of Basic Foods Corporation and partnering with Universal Foods Corporation.

1975

PRODUCTION OF CORN STARCH

GMC expanded into the production of cornstarch.

1982

MANUFACTURE OF CONSUMER PRODUCTS

GMC ventured into the manufacture and sale of consumer products such as snack foods and instant
noodles.

1988

MERGING OF GMC AND BASIC FOODS CORPORATION

Basic Foods Corporation was merged with GMC to become the Fermentation Division.

1989

MANUFACTURE OF “KAFFE DE ORO”

GMC began to manufacture and market coffee products under its own brand, “Kaffe de Oro.”

1989

ESTABLISHED A SOYBEAN EXTRACTION PLANT IN BATANGAS

The Soybean Milling Division was formed with the acquisition of the Phil-Asia Food Industries
complex in Batangas, City from the Asset Privatization Trust.

1998
New President to champion brand equity

GEORGE K. YOUNG CHAIRMAN

George K. Young became Chairman, heralding the company’s new thrust to strengthen its brand equity.

2000
George U. Young, Jr. became Chairman and President

GEORGE U. YOUNG, JR. CHAIRMAN AND PRESIDENT

George U. Young, Jr. became Chairman and President to continue and preserve the goodwill of the
company built over the years.

2004

FEED MILL PLANT IN CDO

GMC established a feed mill plant in Cagayan de Oro City.

2008

50TH FOUNDING ANNIVERSARY

GMC celebrated its 50th founding anniversary.

2015

MONUMENTAL FACILITY UPGRADES

Upgraded Logistics & Port Facilities for Cebu, Davao, and Cagayan de Oro.

2019

INSTALLATION OF FEED MILL 2 PLANT

Completion of another feed mill plant in Lapu-Lapu City, Cebu.

2019
Certified to FSSC 22000 4.1

GMC FLOUR AND BAKERY PRODUCTS FOOD SAFETY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM WAS CERTIFIED TO FSSC 22000 4.1

The certification scope covers Manufacturing of Hard, Soft, and Blended Wheat Flour and Bakery
Products, from receipt of materials, milling of wheat, storage and dispatch of finished products.

2021
Certified to ISO 22000:2018 FSMS

GMC FEED SAFETY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM WAS CERTIFIED TO ISO 22000:2018 FSMS

The certification scope covers Manufacturing of Swine and Poultry feeds in mash, pellet and crumble
form, from receipt of feed ingredient and materials, storage, pelletizing, cooling, packing, and
dispatch of finished products.

2024 2023
Certified to FSSC 22000 4.1

GMC ATTAINED FSSC 22000 VERSION 5.1 FOR ITS FLOUR AND BAKERY INGREDIENTS’ FSMS

The certification scope covers manufacture of milled wheat flour and flour-based bakery ingredients
and premixes

2024

LAPU-LAPU COMPLEX

facility in the country. Built on a sprawling 17-hectare land, GMC Lapu-lapu complex houses flour
milling and feed milling facilities and a hatchery facility. This complex has a wharf that can
accommodate foreign and local vessels.
