GMC PLANTS SEEDS OF SUSTAINABILITY: 2,000 MANGROVE SEEDLINGS PLANTED IN LAPU-LAPU CITY

As part of our continued commitment to environmental stewardship and community partnership, General
Milling
Corporation (GMC) proudly held a Mangrove Planting Activity last March 29, 2025, in Brgy.
Suba-Basbas, Lapu-
Lapu City, in collaboration with the City Environment and Natural Resources Office (CENRO)
Lapu-Lapu.

This meaningful initiative is one of GMC’s Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) efforts aimed at
contributing to
climate action and promoting ecological balance in coastal communities. With the participation of
employee
volunteers and in coordination with CENRO, a total of 2,000 mangrove seedlings were planted,
reinforcing our
role in supporting marine biodiversity, improving coastal protection, and fighting the effects of
climate change.

The mangrove ecosystem plays a vital role in shielding our shorelines from erosion, serving as a
breeding ground
for marine species, and absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. By helping restore and
preserve these
coastal forests, GMC takes an active step toward creating a greener and more sustainable future for
the
generations to come.
